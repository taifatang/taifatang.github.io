Hackathon - Charity Special Effect 2017 Hacker Games

First place winner. Submitted: 27 Jan 2017

First place winner of the Hacker Games from a small team of 5 formed within ASOS.

Special Effect - "We put fun and inclusion back into the lives of people with physical disabilities by helping them to play video games. By using technology ranging from modified joypads to eye-control, we're finding a way for people to play to the very best of their abilities. But we're not just doing it for fun. By levelling the playing field, we're bringing families and friends together and having a profoundly positive impact on therapy, confidence and rehabilitation".

The Hacker Games is a 48-hour competitive hackathon which brings together some of the UKs top engineering teams to address real world issues. Competing teams will be coming from all sectors and tech stacks which will provide a rich mix of ideas and technical solutions.

Omni-Bell

Bell - Written with Java Android Studio. Omni-Bell is an advance entry system capable of converting voice into text, text to voice, take pictures and relay information to Omni-Hub for processing

Omni-Hub

HackerGamesHub - The Omni Hub sits in Azure and controls every part of the home enriching the life of the user and coordinate events from different peripherals. It also offer facial recogintion of images taken by Omni-Bell

Omni-App

Home - Written in Javascript and hosted on AWS it communicate with Omni-Hub to control the open and close of the door. It also convert speech from Omni-Bell to text and displayed it to the app and allowing the user to text back to Omni-Bell.

Omni-Hue

Asos.Hue.Api - Hue library exposes the functionality of Philips Hue as a RestApi. Philips Hue is a lighting system offered by Philips allowing the On/Off and changing of the light remotely.

Asos.Hue.Api.SignalRClient - Hue SignalR Client listens to the Omni-Hub and it reactively flashes using Asos.Hue.Api depending on who is at the door. If he/ she is facially recognised the Hue System will flash Green and Red if not recognised

Created using: C#, Java, Javascript, Cloud
